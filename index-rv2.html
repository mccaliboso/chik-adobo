Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a traditional dish in the filipino culture with simple essential ingredients. These simple ingredients are soy sauce or equivalent, white vinegar, black pepper, garlic and bay leaves. Each ingredient listed are essential and is what makes the chicken adobo unique.

However, the ratio of each ingredient in the recipe differs from each region of the Philippines and the preferences of each individual and families. I prefer to use clean dark meat for my recipe so I generally use skinless boneless chicken thighs.

Every ingredient in this receipe is keto friendly and serves about 5 individuals. The only thing very different in the reciepe is the Bouquet Garni which I discovered while making a French cuisine. It certainly adds another element of flavor to Chicken Adobo.

Ingredients

Chicken Adobo

  • 4 to 5 lbs Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • 1/4 cup Liquid Amino
  • 2 tablespoon Oyster Sauce
  • 3 tablespoon Erythritol
  • 5 cloves Garlic
  • 2 teaspoon Ground Black Pepper
  • 1/2 cup White Vinegar
  • 1/4 cup Apple Cider Vinegar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 Bouquet Garni

Bouquet Garni

Bouquet Garni is a bunch of herbs, typically encased in a cheesecloth bag or bound together with cooking twine. I used cooking twine to bind the herbs together.

  • 1 sprig Thyme
  • 2 sprig Rosemary
  • 2 Stalks Parsley
  • 2 Bay Leaves

Cooking Directions

  • Cut the boneless skinless chicken thighs into one inch cubes.
  • Mix the Garlic Paste and Ground Black Pepper with the chicken.
  • Add the Liquid Amino, Oyster Sauce and White Vinegar to the chicken in the pot and mix.
  • Place the Erythritol on top of the chicken with the one cup of water and mix.
  • Place the Bouquet Garni on one side and make sure it is submerged.
  • Cook in medium heat until it starts to simmer, then lower heat to medium low heat.
  • Allow ingredients to cook for 50 minutes covered.

    • The temperature of the stove may vary from person to person so additional cooking time may be required. The adobo is cooked when most of the sauce has been absorbed into the chicken and the oil from the chicken covers the chicken.
